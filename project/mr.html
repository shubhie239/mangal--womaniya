Minal Rohit


Minal Rohit started her career as a Satellite Communications Engineer at Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Later, she went on to work for the Space Application Centre.



Systems Engineer and project manager

Minal Rohit was one of the driving forces behind the Mangalyaan mission where India made it to Mars in their first attempt. She is a scientist and systems engineer working with the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). As Systems Engineer for the Mangalyaan mission, she helped integrate and test the sensors that the orbiter was carrying.


Beginnings of Space Dreams

She dreamt of becoming a scientist after watching a space show as a child where people were seen in white coloured dresses working on the technology. Her peers only went for professions where there was scope for women including good salary. She was the only woman from her social circle who got full education including college.


Experience at ISRO

Minal started as a Satellite Communications Engineer at Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and eventually became one of the 500 scientists working on Mangalyaan. Her job was to integrate and test the sensors that the Mars orbiter was carrying. She is Deputy Project Director at ISRO and hoped to be a director to head a national space agency and break the glass ceiling as a woman.


Research contributions

As a senior engineer at the ISRO, Minal Rohit served as the Project Manager as well as Systems Engineer. She was primarily involved with incorporating the components of the methane sensor (MSM), Lyman-alpha Photometer (LAP), Thermal Infrared Imaging Spectrometer (TIS), and Mars Colour Camera (MCC) onto the orbiter.


Extreme dedication to work

Minal Rohit was one of the key faces in the successful accomplishment of the Mars Orbiter Mission. She was the Project Manager and Head Engineer in that mission. Rohit and her team confined themselves in a room and worked for around 18 hours a day. The team was working to make methane sensors for the mars mission.
